\section{Preliminary Results}
This section summarizes early outcomes from the current experiment matrix and one-hour benchmark suite. The results are preliminary and are included to validate feasibility, instrumentation coverage, and the expected analysis pipeline for the full thesis study.

\subsection{High-Level Observations}
Across the current runs, p99 latency increases with noise intensity for all isolation methods. Low-noise conditions show comparatively tight tails, while medium and high-noise settings produce clear tail amplification. The early comparisons also show that method rankings are scenario-dependent: some methods perform better under specific traffic/failure combinations, but no single method dominates all cases in this preliminary dataset.

\subsection{Threats to Validity (Preliminary Stage)}
These findings are preliminary and may still be influenced by run-to-run variance, incomplete repetition counts for some scenarios, and pending stabilization of adaptive cgroup-map update behavior in selected runs. The final thesis analysis will use controlled reruns, confidence-aware reporting, and explicit exclusion/annotation criteria for incomplete trials.
